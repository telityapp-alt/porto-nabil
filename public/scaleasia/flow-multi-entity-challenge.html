Multi-Entity Operations Challenge
Running businesses across Asia means juggling 5+ legal entities, each with different compliance rules
Problem
Operational Fragmentation at Scale
Companies expanding in Asia quickly hit operational complexity wall. Each jurisdiction requires separate legal entity, compliance calendar, tax filing, banking relationship, and hiring process. No unified playbook exists. Operators cobble together consultant advice, government PDFs, and Reddit threads.
Typical Multi-Entity Setup
  • Singapore HQ (Pte Ltd) for regional base and banking hub
  • Indonesia PT PMA (foreign investment company) for local operations
  • Philippines ROHQ (regional operating headquarters) for shared services
  • India Private Limited for tech and product development
  • Japan KK (kabushiki kaisha) for enterprise sales and partnerships
Compliance Burden Per Entity
  • Annual filings: AGM resolutions, financial statements, director declarations (deadlines vary by jurisdiction)
  • Tax obligations: Corporate tax, withholding tax, GST/VAT, transfer pricing documentation
  • Statutory audits: Mandatory for certain entity types and revenue thresholds
  • Beneficial ownership: UBO (ultimate beneficial owner) disclosure with periodic updates
  • Employment compliance: Payroll tax, social security, labor law updates
  • Banking KYC: Annual review, source of funds documentation, regulatory reporting
Cost
Hidden Operational Drag
Compliance is not just financial cost. It is operational drag on finance and legal teams. Every deadline missed risks penalties. Every regulation change requires context translation (what does this mean for us?). Every new hire in a new jurisdiction requires local employment law research.
Annual Spend Breakdown (5-entity setup)
  • Legal & compliance: $40K-80K/year (corporate secretary, annual filings, audit fees)
  • Accounting & tax: $30K-60K/year (bookkeeping, tax prep, transfer pricing reports)
  • Banking fees: $10K-25K/year (account maintenance, FX spread, wire fees)
  • Consultants on-demand: $20K-50K/year (ad-hoc questions, structure reviews, M&A prep)
  • Internal time cost: 20-40% of finance/legal headcount on compliance busywork
Failure Modes
What Goes Wrong
Operators learn the hard way. Missed AGM filing → penalty + rush fee to file late. Wrong employment classification → back taxes + interest. Beneficial ownership not updated → bank freeze account pending compliance. Transfer pricing documentation missing → tax authority audit. These are not edge cases — they are routine when scaling multi-entity operations.
Indonesia
PT PMA beneficial ownership disclosure now mandatory — miss it and face frozen bank accounts until compliance
Singapore
AGM within 6 months of FYE, AR within 7 months — late filing compounds daily
Philippines
BIR tax filing extensions require pre-approval — miss deadline and pay 25% surcharge + 20% interest
India
Transfer pricing documentation for related-party transactions — tax authority presumes non-compliance without it
Malaysia
SSM (company registry) annual return + financial statements — RM5K penalty + possible striking off for persistent default
Japan
Residence tax pre-payment even at zero profit — no exemption, file or face compounding penalties
Information Gap
No Single Source of Truth
Existing resources fall short. Government websites list rules but not context (what triggers this? when does it apply?). Big 4 consultants charge $500/hour for questions you could answer yourself with the right playbook. Reddit and founder forums have anecdotes but no structure. No platform consolidates operator-tested tactics for multi-entity compliance, hiring, banking, and tax across Asia.
What Operators Actually Need
  • Compliance calendars: Entity-specific deadlines with lead time and document checklists
  • Regulatory updates: New rules translated to action items (file X by Y, update Z in entity docs)
  • Operator playbooks: Real tactics from teams running the same setup (how we handle transfer pricing, how we structure equity across entities)
  • Decision trees: Should we use ROHQ or branch in Philippines? PT vs KPPA in Indonesia? Context-sensitive guidance
  • Vendor benchmarks: Corporate secretary pricing, audit firm recommendations, banking partner comparisons
Why This Problem Persists
Consultants have no incentive to productize knowledge — billable hours model rewards complexity. SaaS tools focus on single-jurisdiction workflows (US payroll, UK incorporation) and ignore multi-entity Asia operations. Government portals are compliance-first, not operator-friendly. The gap is not tooling — it is curated, operator-tested intelligence structured for teams already running multi-entity businesses in Asia.
